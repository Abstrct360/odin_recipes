Recipe description

Greek honey cake is a traditional Mediterranean dessert known for its soft, moist texture and rich aromatic flavor. Sweetened naturally with honey and often infused with citrus, cinnamon, or warm spices, this cake reflects the simplicity of Greek home baking. After baking, the cake is typically soaked in a fragrant honey syrup, giving it a delicate sweetness and a tender crumb. It is commonly served with chopped nuts such as walnuts or almonds and enjoyed with coffee or tea.

    Ingredients
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Preparation Steps

    Reheat oven to 180°C (350°F).Grease and lightly flour a baking pan.

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients

    2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.

  2. Prepare Wet Mixture

    3. In a separate bowl, beat eggs and sugar until light and slightly fluffy. Add olive oil (or butter), yogurt, milk, vanilla, and citrus zest.Mix well.

  3. Combine Mixtures

    4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring gently until a smooth batter forms. Avoid overmixing.

  4. Bake the Cake

    5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 30–40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

  5. Prepare the Honey Syrup

    6. While the cake bakes, combine honey, water, sugar, cinnamon stick, and citrus peel in a saucepan. Simmer for 5–7 minutes until slightly thickened. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.

  6. Soak the Cake

    7. Once the cake is baked and still warm, slowly pour the warm syrup evenly over the surface, allowing it to absorb fully.

  7. Garnish and Serve

    8. Sprinkle chopped nuts on top and drizzle with extra honey if desired. Allow the cake to rest for at least 20 minutes before serving.
