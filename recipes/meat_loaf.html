Recipe description

Meatloaf is a comforting, traditional dish made from seasoned ground meat shaped into a loaf and baked until tender and flavorful. Popular in many households, it combines minced meat with breadcrumbs, herbs, and spices to create a moist, hearty meal. Topped with a savory tomato-based glaze, meatloaf offers a balance of rich flavor and soft texture. It is commonly served with mashed potatoes, vegetables, or salad, making it a satisfying family-style main course.

    Ingredients

  • 500 g (1 lb) ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley or mixed herbs
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil (for sautéing)

    • For the Glaze:

  • ⅓ cup ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon mustard (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Preheat the Oven

    2. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Lightly grease a baking tray or loaf pan.

  2. Prepare the Aromatics

    3. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the chopped onion for 3–4 minutes until soft. Add garlic and cook for another minute. Allow to cool slightly.

  3. Combine Ingredients

    4. In a large bowl, mix ground meat, breadcrumbs, egg, milk, sautéed onion and garlic, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, and herbs.

  4. Shape the Loaf

    5. Transfer the mixture to the baking tray or loaf pan and shape it into a compact loaf.

  5. Prepare the Glaze

    6. In a small bowl, mix ketchup, brown sugar, and mustard. Spread half of the glaze evenly over the top of the meatloaf.

  6. Bake

    7. Bake for 45–55 minutes, or until fully cooked and the internal temperature reaches about 70–75°C (160–165°F).

  7. Add Final Glaze

    8. Remove from the oven, spread the remaining glaze on top, and return to the oven for an additional 5–10 minutes.

  8. Rest and Serve

    9. Allow the meatloaf to rest for 10 minutes before slicing to help retain its juices.
